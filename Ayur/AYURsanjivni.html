Causes of Knee Pain and Osteoarthritis

Osteoarthritis (OA)
The most prevalent cause of knee pain is osteoarthritis. It occurs due to the gradual wear and tear of the knee joint’s cartilage. As the cartilage deteriorates, the bones may rub against each other, leading to pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility.
Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
In cases of rheumatoid arthritis, an autoimmune disorder, the body’s immune system attacks the synovium (lining of the membranes that surround the joints). This can result in inflammation, pain, and damage to the knee joint.
Traumatic Injuries
Knee pain can also result from traumatic injuries such as ligament tears, meniscus tears, or fractures. These injuries can cause acute pain and may lead to long-term issues if not treated promptly and effectively.
Overuse and Repetitive Stress
Engaging in activities that involve repetitive knee movements, such as sports or certain occupations, can lead to overuse injuries. This includes conditions like patellar tendinitis, where the patellar tendon becomes inflamed.
